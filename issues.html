Warm laps

When sweaters aren't enough, warm laps are a great defense against the cold. Friends who are willing to share their warmth with the defenseless are encouraged to adopt a pet today!

Caring for a companion animal goes far beyond providing food, water and shelter. It takes research and careful planning to bring the right pet into your home, and to make sure your lifestyle is the right one for your pet. Read on for tips to prepare yourself, as well as your home, for a new furry friend.

Sweaters

Some dogs need a little help in the warmth department!

They obviously can’t keep themselves warm so if you stick to your belief that dogs can surpass those hard times, then maybe you need to think again. There are cases that even with their thick fur and body fats, some dogs still die when they are out in the snow. Your pooch is lucky to be adopted and kept safe and warm inside the comforts of your home. Giving it an extra sweater matters a lot. Extra hot weathers also poses a threat to your pet’s skin but this time they do not need an extra piece of clothing. However, when your pooch is also experiencing hair loss exposing its bare skin to the sun is not advisable and this time a piece of sun shirt would be very much needed.

Animal Neglect

The ASPCA’s efforts to protect animals span from federal laws to local courts to community engagement. We’re helping to prevent harmful situations for animals and to take action when they occur.

No matter the location or scale of the deployment, the ASPCA is prepared to take on animal cruelty cases when needed—from initial investigations to the final placement of rescued animals in homes.
